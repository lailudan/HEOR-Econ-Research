## 01. The "Goldin Weapon": WNBA Bargaining Model
> *Case Study: How Claudia Goldin (2023 Nobel Laureate) used high-level Econometrics to secure a 400% pay rise for WNBA players.*

### Core Frameworks
1. **Nash Bargaining Model ($u$ & $d$)**
   The power dynamic is defined by the "Disagreement Point" ($d$). By modeling long-term revenue trends and media rights growth, the players' threat point was elevated.
   $$\max_{x} (u_{players}(x) - d_{players})^\alpha (u_{league}(x) - d_{league})^{1-\alpha}$$

2. **Revenue Sharing Model**
   Shifting from fixed salaries to "equity-style" distributions. This model ties compensation directly to the marginal contribution of players to the league's growth (Broadcasting, Merchandising).

3. **"Greedy Work" Theory**
   Re-evaluating high-pressure, irregular-hour roles. Goldin proved that the "flexibility penalty" in professional sports was a mispricing of human capital.

**Takeaway for HEOR:** This is "Value Re-valuation." In Health Economics, we use similar models to negotiate drug pricing by proving the long-term QALY gains and societal cost reduction.
